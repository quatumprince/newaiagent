But dig into the data, and you see a fascinating trend: the use of **stablecoins** as collateral has shifted from being a minor player or secondary option to becoming the dominant force recently.

**Stablecoins** like USDT, USDC, and DAI are designed to be pegged 1:1 to traditional assets like the US Dollar. Moreover, Their key advantage is stability ‚Äì see, you're collateralizing with something less volatile, which simplifies things for the trading platforms and potentially lowers some counterparty risk (at least *on paper*). Plus, it makes capital deployment much easier for holders of stablecoins. If you've accumulated stablecoins from selling some crypto, earning interest on a lending pool, or just general accumulation (via sites like NFTGo or Arkham scanning holdings), you can now often park them in a lending protocol (like Aave or Compound) and use the resulting stablecoin debt position as collateral for leveraged trades in derivatives (like on Bybit or BitMEX).

**(Analysis Point - Internal Link):** This shift towards stablecoin collateral reflects a maturing infrastructure, similar to how traditional finance uses lines of credit. It allows users to move seamlessly between holding stable value and deploying it into higher-risk/higher-return markets like the crypto derivatives space.
